# Strategy Consultant Skill

Structured business analysis and strategic recommendations.

## When to Use
- "Analyze my business idea"
- "Do a SWOT analysis for [company/product]"
- "What's the market size for [X]?"
- "Help me plan a go-to-market strategy"
- "Compare our approach to competitors"

## Frameworks (choose based on question)

### SWOT Analysis
| | Positive | Negative |
|---|---------|----------|
| Internal | **Strengths** | **Weaknesses** |
| External | **Opportunities** | **Threats** |

### Porter's Five Forces
1. Competitive rivalry
2. Supplier power
3. Buyer power
4. Threat of substitution
5. Threat of new entry

### TAM/SAM/SOM (Market Sizing)
- **TAM**: Total addressable market (everyone who could use this)
- **SAM**: Serviceable available market (your reachable segment)
- **SOM**: Serviceable obtainable market (realistic year-1 capture)

### Go-to-Market Canvas
1. Target customer (specific persona)
2. Value proposition (1 sentence)
3. Channel strategy (how to reach them)
4. Pricing model
5. Key metrics (what to measure)
6. Unfair advantage (what's hard to copy)

## Workflow

### Phase 1: Context Gathering
Ask clarifying questions if needed:
- What's the business/product?
- What stage? (idea / MVP / growth / mature)
- Who's the target customer?
- What's the competitive landscape?

### Phase 2: Research
Use `web_search` to find:
- Market size data, growth rates
- Competitor analysis (who, pricing, positioning)
- Industry trends and reports
- Regulatory considerations

### Phase 3: Analysis
Apply the relevant framework(s) and produce:
1. Situation assessment
2. Key insights (3-5, data-backed)
3. Strategic options (2-3 paths)
4. Recommended path with rationale
5. Risk factors and mitigations
6. 90-day action plan

### Output
Structured report with framework visuals, data tables, and clear recommendations.
Always end with: "**Next step:** [single most important action to take now]"
